# ethics_policy.md
## The Artist Signal Engine: research and editorial ethics

This file is law for every part of The Arts Wire that gathers, reads, or writes. The code references it; the humans honor it. Style rule: no em dashes anywhere.

We listen to the public life of art so we can give it back to artists with meaning. We are not a shovel. We are an ear.

### What we collect
Public cultural signals only: published arts journalism, public artist interviews and statements, public talks and podcasts with public feeds, open museum and archive data, open-access scholarship, public calendars, open calls, grants, residencies, festival listings, and open-license or public-domain material.

### What we do not collect
Anything behind an access control. No private groups, no private Discords or Facebook groups, no direct messages, no member-only posts, no paywalled articles without a license, no scraped social pages, nothing that bypasses a login or a paywall. We do not build profiles of private individuals, and we do not publish personal data.

### What we cite
Everything. Every signal carries its source and link. Every essay credits the voices and works it draws on. Credit generously by default, even when not strictly required.

### What we summarize
We summarize in our own words, always. Short quotations are used only when necessary and clearly attributed. We never reproduce full articles, and we never lean so close to a source's wording or structure that our summary could stand in for the original.

### What we refuse
We refuse to imitate the style of any living writer. We refuse to invent quotes or fabricate private experience. We refuse to exploit personal vulnerability for engagement. We refuse to turn human communication into cold extraction. When a request crosses any of these lines, the answer is no, and the reason is stated plainly.

### How we protect artists
We treat artists as people, not data. We listen for patterns across many public voices rather than profiling any single private person. We use the tiered source system below. We keep a human, Rey, in final control of everything that publishes. When in doubt, we choose the artist's dignity over the click.

### Source tiers
Green, preferred and low risk: RSS feeds, official APIs, open museum APIs, public-domain collections, verified Creative Commons material, open-access scholarship, public podcast feeds, permitted official video metadata and transcripts, public calendars, public open calls and listings, and public artist statements used with a link and at most a short quote.

Yellow, allowed only after checking terms, robots rules, and rate limits: public social media through official APIs only; public newsletters only if they have public archives; websites without a feed only if robots.txt and terms allow; public PDFs with citation and short excerpts; public comments only for broad pattern recognition, never personal profiling.

Red, forbidden: private groups, private Discords, private Facebook groups, direct messages, member-only posts, paywalled articles without a license, scraped social pages, anything that bypasses access controls, full copyrighted articles, downloaded copyrighted images without permission, imitation of a living writer's style, profiles of vulnerable individuals, publication of personal data, and use of an artist's words as raw material without credit or transformation.

### The one test
Before anything is gathered, written, or published, ask: does this help an artist understand, survive, and keep making, and does it honor the person it came from. If not, we do not do it, however useful it would be.
